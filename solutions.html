Disinfection of the air using ultraviolet technology is being recognized as one of the most effective and economical methods to fight Covid by different national and international organizations.


There are two systems that are most installed for the purification of viruses and bacteria from the air in closed spaces:


Air purification taking advantage of existing air conditioning installations. In this case, depending on the type of air conditioning installation, different solutions may be chosen, in most cases with the incorporation of ultraviolet lights inside the air conditioning ducts where air recirculation occurs.

Forced ventilation. Recommended system for closed rooms where there are no air conditioning installations or that require greater disinfection power in the surrounding air, such as in the kitchen, food industry and spaces where the accumulation of people she's very tall. It is also recommended when the air conditioning installation does not have a heat pump for winter.

Therefore, the design of the solution for air disinfection will be carried out according to the needs in volume of air to be treated and the ventilation technology that already exists in the space that we want to condition.


Some solutions allow air and surfaces to be treated at the same time. Ask us about the most effective solution in each specific case.

Air Disinfection With UV Lamps

Ultraviolet Lamp Installations for UV disinfection

The design of our solutions is based on the appropriate ultraviolet disinfection power for each space. For this, different models of ultraviolet devices are used that can be installed on the wall or with their own base. In this way, maximum versatility is achieved in professional installations and with the price adjusted to the needs of each specific space.


The time that uv lamps have to be on depends on the size of the space to be disinfected and the power of the ultraviolet device model.


Another type of solutions consists in the installation of units of ultraviolet lamps distributed regularly on the ceiling of the room and that allow a deep disinfection of air, floors, walls and surfaces with long exposures during the night (45-60 minutes) with doses that ensure 99% inactivation even in areas not directly irradiated thanks to the action of direct and reflected UV-C radiation.
